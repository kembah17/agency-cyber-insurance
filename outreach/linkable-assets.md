## 1. Existing Content Linkability Analysis

Our current content library: **21 articles (~126,000 words)** plus the **Cyber Insurance Recommendation Engine**.

Linkability is scored on five factors:
- **Data Originality** — Does it contain unique data, statistics, or analysis not found elsewhere?
- **Reference Value** — Would other writers cite this as a source when covering the topic?
- **Evergreen Potential** — Will this remain relevant and accurate for 12+ months?
- **Visual/Embed Potential** — Can elements be embedded, screenshotted, or referenced visually?
- **Outreach Fit** — Does it match what target publications need (resource pages, expert roundups, etc.)?

## 3. New Linkable Asset Ideas

### Asset #1: Cyber Insurance Cost Calculator (Interactive Tool)

**Format:** Interactive web tool (JavaScript-based, client-side)
**Estimated build time:** 2–3 days
**Estimated cost:** $0 (built in-house with Next.js)

**Concept:**
A calculator that estimates cyber insurance premiums based on inputs: industry, employee count, revenue, data types handled, existing security controls, and desired coverage limits. Outputs a premium range with breakdown of factors affecting price.

**Why it will earn links:**
- Calculators are the #1 most-linked tool type in financial content (Ahrefs data)
- No free, non-lead-gen cyber insurance calculator exists
- Every article about cyber insurance costs needs a "calculate your estimate" CTA
- Journalists and bloggers embed calculator links as reader resources
- Complements our existing cost guide (cross-linking opportunity)

**Data foundation:** Our existing pricing research across six carriers provides the underlying model:
- Solo/freelancer (1–5 employees): $500–$1,500/year
- Small agency (5–10 employees): $1,500–$4,000/year
- Mid-size agency (11–50 employees): $3,000–$12,000/year
- Larger agency (51–250 employees): $10,000–$50,000/year
- Modifiers: industry (+20–40% for healthcare data), security controls (-30–40% combined), bundling (-15–25%), deductible choice (-15–20%)

**Pitch strategy:**
- Submit to Product Hunt alongside recommendation engine
- Pitch to resource pages: "Free cyber insurance cost calculator — no email required"
- Include in guest posts: "We built a free calculator to estimate your premium — [link]"
- HARO responses: "Based on our calculator data, the average small business pays..."

**Expected links:** 12–20 in first 6 months (calculators have the highest link velocity of any tool type)

### Asset #3: Cyber Insurance Claims Map (Interactive Visualization)

**Format:** Interactive data visualization (D3.js or Chart.js embedded in Next.js page)
**Estimated build time:** 3–4 days
**Estimated cost:** $0 (built in-house)

**Concept:**
An interactive visualization showing cyber insurance claims data broken down by:
- Claim type (funds transfer fraud, ransomware, data breach, business interruption)
- Industry sector
- Company size
- Average claim amount
- Year-over-year trends

Users can filter and explore the data. Key insight highlighted: funds transfer fraud at 29–39% is the #1 claim type, contradicting the ransomware-dominated narrative.

**Why it will earn links:**
- Interactive visualizations earn 3–4x more links than static content (BuzzSumo data)
- Visual content gets embedded in presentations, reports, and blog posts
- The "funds transfer fraud > ransomware" narrative is counterintuitive and shareable
- Cybersecurity publications love data visualizations
- Can be screenshotted and shared on social media with attribution

**Data sources:**
- Aggregated from public carrier reports (Coalition, At-Bay, Corvus annual reports)
- FBI IC3 annual reports
- Verizon DBIR data
- Our own research and analysis

**Pitch strategy:**
- Cybersecurity publications: "Interactive claims data visualization — free to embed with attribution"
- Insurance publications: "Visual breakdown of cyber insurance claims trends"
- Guest posts: Reference specific data points with link to interactive version
- Social media: Share screenshots of key findings with link to full interactive
- Offer embed code for other sites to include the visualization

**Expected links:** 10–15 in first 6 months

**Pitch strategy:**

### Asset #5: Agency Cyber Risk Assessment Scorecard (Interactive)

**Format:** Interactive assessment tool (Next.js, client-side) + downloadable PDF report
**Estimated build time:** 3–4 days
**Estimated cost:** $0 (built in-house)

**Concept:**
A 15-question assessment that evaluates a digital agency's cyber risk profile across five categories:
1. **Data Exposure** — What client data do you handle? (ad accounts, analytics, PII, payment info)
2. **Access Controls** — MFA, password management, access reviews
3. **Technical Controls** — EDR, backups, patching, email authentication
4. **Process Controls** — Payment verification, incident response plan, employee training
5. **Insurance Readiness** — Current coverage, policy review frequency, claims preparedness

Outputs a scored report card (A–F per category) with specific recommendations and links to relevant guides on our site.

**Why it will earn links:**
- Assessment tools have high engagement (people love scoring themselves)
- Generates shareable results ("I scored a B+ on the Agency Cyber Risk Assessment")
- MSPs and consultants will use it with clients and link to it as a resource
- Complements the recommendation engine (assess risk → get insurance recommendation)
- Results page naturally links to our relevant content (internal link boost)

**Pitch strategy:**
- Agency blogs: "Free cyber risk assessment built specifically for digital agencies"
- MSP blogs: "A risk assessment tool you can use with your agency clients"
- Cybersecurity blogs: "Interactive tool mapping security controls to insurance readiness"
- LinkedIn: "Take the 5-minute Agency Cyber Risk Assessment" (high engagement post format)
- Guest posts: "We built a free risk assessment — here's what we learned from the data"

**Expected links:** 10–15 in first 6 months

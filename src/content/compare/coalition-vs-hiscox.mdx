## Coalition: In-Depth Review

Coalition has positioned itself as the "insurtech" leader in cyber insurance. Founded in 2017, they've quickly become one of the largest cyber insurance providers in North America.

### What Sets Coalition Apart

Coalition's biggest differentiator is their **active risk monitoring**. When you purchase a policy, Coalition continuously scans your digital infrastructure for vulnerabilities and alerts you before they become incidents. This proactive approach is genuinely valuable for agencies that may not have dedicated IT security staff.

Their platform also provides:
- **Coalition Control** — A dashboard showing your risk score and security posture
- **Automated alerts** — Notifications about exposed credentials, vulnerable software, and misconfigured services
- **Security recommendations** — Actionable steps to reduce your risk (and potentially your premium)

### Coalition Coverage Details

Coalition's policy is comprehensive and specifically designed for technology businesses:

- **First-party coverage:** Breach response, business interruption, digital asset restoration, cyber extortion, social engineering fraud
- **Third-party coverage:** Network security liability, privacy liability, regulatory proceedings, media liability
- **Coverage limits:** Up to $15M (most agencies will need $1M-$5M)
- **Deductibles:** Starting at $1,000 for small businesses

### Coalition Pricing for Agencies

Based on our research and community reports, here's what agencies typically pay:

- **5-person agency, $500K revenue:** $1,200 - $1,800/year
- **15-person agency, $2M revenue:** $2,500 - $4,500/year
- **30-person agency, $5M revenue:** $5,000 - $9,000/year

*Actual pricing depends on your specific risk profile, security measures, and claims history.*

## Hiscox: In-Depth Review

Hiscox is a well-established insurer (founded 1901) that has built a strong reputation in the small business insurance market. Their cyber insurance product is straightforward and competitively priced.

### What Sets Hiscox Apart

Hiscox's strength is **simplicity and affordability**. Their online quote process is one of the fastest in the industry, and their policies are written in relatively plain language. For small agencies that want solid coverage without complexity, Hiscox is an excellent choice.

### Hiscox Coverage Details

- **First-party coverage:** Data breach response, business interruption, cyber extortion, data recovery
- **Third-party coverage:** Network security liability, privacy liability, regulatory defense
- **Coverage limits:** Up to $5M
- **Deductibles:** Starting at $1,000

### Hiscox Pricing for Agencies

- **5-person agency, $500K revenue:** $800 - $1,400/year
- **15-person agency, $2M revenue:** $1,800 - $3,200/year
- **30-person agency, $5M revenue:** $3,500 - $6,500/year

## Head-to-Head: Key Differences

### Coverage Breadth

**Winner: Coalition**

Coalition offers broader coverage, particularly for social engineering fraud and funds transfer fraud — two of the most common threats to agencies. Hiscox's social engineering coverage is more limited and may require an endorsement.

### Pricing

**Winner: Hiscox**

Hiscox is generally 20-30% less expensive than Coalition for comparable coverage limits. If budget is your primary concern, Hiscox offers strong value.

### Technology & Risk Management

**Winner: Coalition**

Coalition's active risk monitoring is a genuine differentiator. No other insurer provides this level of proactive security support included with the policy.

### Claims Process

**Tie**

Both providers have generally positive claims reviews. Coalition's 24/7 incident response is an advantage for after-hours incidents, while Hiscox's established claims infrastructure provides reliability.

### Ease of Purchase

**Winner: Hiscox**

Hiscox's quote process is slightly faster and simpler. Coalition asks more detailed questions about your technology stack, which provides better coverage but takes more time.

## Our Recommendation

For most digital agencies, we recommend **Coalition** as the better choice. The active risk monitoring alone justifies the modest price premium, and the broader coverage — particularly for social engineering — addresses the threats agencies face most frequently.

However, **Hiscox is the better choice if:**
- You're a very small agency (1-3 people) with a tight budget
- You want the simplest possible purchasing experience
- You already have robust security monitoring in place
- You primarily need coverage for client contract requirements

## Getting Started

Both providers offer quick online quotes with no obligation:

- **Coalition:** Get a quote in about 10 minutes. You'll need basic business information and details about your technology stack.
- **Hiscox:** Get a quote in about 8 minutes. The process is straightforward with minimal technical questions.

We recommend getting quotes from both providers and comparing the specific coverage terms for your agency's situation.

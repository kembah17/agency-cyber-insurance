When we started narrowing down our cyber insurance shortlist, two names kept surfacing in every conversation with agency owners: **Embroker** and **Coalition**. Both are digital-first insurers built for tech companies, both offer online quotes in minutes, and both understand the risks agencies face every day.

But they take fundamentally different approaches to cyber insurance — and those differences matter when you're choosing the policy that will protect your agency if something goes wrong.

We spent weeks comparing these two providers side by side: getting quotes, testing their platforms, reading policy documents, and talking to agencies that have filed claims with each. Here's what we found.

## Embroker: The Tech-Focused Budget Pick

Embroker was built from the ground up for startups and technology companies. Unlike legacy insurers that bolt cyber coverage onto traditional business policies, Embroker designed its entire platform around the risks that digital businesses actually face.

### What Makes Embroker Different

Embroker's core advantage is its **streamlined digital experience**. When we went through their quote process, we had a bindable quote in about 12 minutes — no phone calls, no waiting for an underwriter to get back to us three days later. The application asks smart questions about your tech stack, client data handling, and security practices, then generates a tailored policy.

Their underwriting team genuinely understands technology companies. When we described our agency's workflow — managing client CMS platforms, handling analytics data, running ad accounts with significant spend — the coverage recommendations made sense. We didn't have to explain what a staging environment was or why we have SSH access to client servers.

### Embroker Coverage Details

- **Coverage limits:** Up to $10M
- **Starting price:** Approximately $80/month for small agencies
- **Key coverages:** Data breach response, business interruption, cyber extortion, media liability, regulatory defense
- **Deductibles:** Typically $2,500–$5,000 for small agencies
- **Policy management:** Fully digital dashboard for certificates, claims, and renewals

### Embroker Pros

- **Digital-first platform** — Quote to bind entirely online, no broker required
- **Competitive pricing** — Consistently 15–20% lower than comparable providers for small agencies
- **Tech company expertise** — Underwriters who understand SaaS, agencies, and digital businesses
- **Fast quote process** — Bindable quotes in roughly 12 minutes
- **Bundling options** — Can package cyber with E&O, D&O, and general liability

### Embroker Cons

- **Less established brand** — Newer entrant compared to legacy carriers
- **Fewer coverage customization options** — Less flexibility on sublimits and endorsements
- **No active monitoring** — Provides security resources but no continuous scanning
- **Business hours incident response** — No 24/7 hotline for active breaches
- **Lower maximum limits** — $10M ceiling may not suit larger agencies

## Coalition: The Active Insurance Pioneer

Coalition pioneered what they call "Active Insurance" — the idea that an insurer shouldn't just pay claims after something goes wrong, but should actively help prevent incidents from happening in the first place. It's a fundamentally different philosophy, and it shows in everything they build.

### What Makes Coalition Different

The standout feature is **Coalition Control**, their proprietary risk monitoring platform. The moment you get a quote — before you even buy a policy — Coalition scans your digital footprint and shows you exactly what vulnerabilities they found. When we ran our agency through it, Coalition identified an exposed admin login page, two services running outdated TLS configurations, and a subdomain with a known CVE.

That's not a gimmick. Coalition Control runs continuously throughout your policy term, sending automated alerts when new vulnerabilities appear, credentials get exposed in data breaches, or your security posture changes. For agencies managing dozens of client domains and subdomains, this kind of monitoring is genuinely valuable.

### Coalition Coverage Details

- **Coverage limits:** Up to $15M

- **Deductibles:** Typically $1,000–$5,000 depending on coverage and agency size
- **Incident response:** 24/7 hotline with pre-vetted forensics, legal, and crisis management teams
- **Coalition Control:** Continuous vulnerability scanning, risk scoring, dark web monitoring, and security recommendations

### Coalition Pros

- **Active monitoring via Coalition Control** — Continuous scanning, risk scores, and automated alerts
- **Broadest coverage** — Includes social engineering, funds transfer fraud, and cryptojacking
- **24/7 incident response** — Pre-vetted team available around the clock, not just business hours
- **Fast automated underwriting** — Bindable quotes in approximately 10 minutes
- **Higher limits available** — Up to $15M for agencies that need robust coverage

### Coalition Cons

## Head-to-Head: Five Key Comparisons

### 1. Pricing

**Winner: Embroker**

Embroker consistently comes in about 20% cheaper than Coalition for comparable coverage levels. Here's what we found when we quoted both for different agency sizes:

| Solo freelancer | $150K | ~$600 | ~$780 | Embroker saves $180 |
| Small agency (5 people) | $750K | ~$960 | ~$1,200 | Embroker saves $240 |
| Mid-size agency (20 people) | $3M | ~$2,400 | ~$3,000 | Embroker saves $600 |
| Larger agency (50 people) | $8M | ~$5,500 | ~$7,200 | Embroker saves $1,700 |

If budget is your primary constraint, Embroker delivers solid coverage at a lower price point. But as we'll see in the next sections, Coalition's premium buys you meaningfully more.

### 2. Coverage Breadth

**Winner: Coalition**

Both providers cover the essentials: data breach response, business interruption, cyber extortion, and regulatory defense. But Coalition pulls ahead with broader coverage for the threats that actually hit agencies hardest.

**Social engineering coverage** is the biggest differentiator. Business email compromise (BEC) attacks — where an attacker impersonates a client or vendor to redirect payments — cost businesses an average of $200,000–$300,000 per incident. Coalition includes robust social engineering coverage as standard. Embroker offers it, but with tighter sublimits.

**Funds transfer fraud** is another area where Coalition leads. If an attacker compromises your email and redirects a client payment, Coalition's policy covers the loss. This matters for agencies that handle media buying budgets or pass through significant client spend.

Coalition also covers **cryptojacking** (unauthorized use of your systems for cryptocurrency mining) and offers broader **contingent business interruption** coverage for when a vendor or cloud provider you depend on gets breached.

### 3. Platform Experience

**Winner: Tie (slight edge to Coalition)**

Both Embroker and Coalition are genuinely digital-first. Neither requires you to call a broker, wait for paper documents, or fax anything (yes, some insurers still ask for faxes in 2025).

Embroker's dashboard is clean and straightforward — you can manage policies, download certificates of insurance, and file claims online. The quote process is intuitive and doesn't require insurance expertise to navigate.

Coalition's platform does everything Embroker's does, plus it integrates Coalition Control directly into your dashboard. You get a real-time risk score, a list of active vulnerabilities, and actionable recommendations — all without leaving the insurance portal. For agencies that want their insurance to do more than sit in a drawer until something goes wrong, Coalition's platform is more compelling.

### 4. Risk Monitoring

**Winner: Coalition (decisively)**

This is where the comparison becomes lopsided. Embroker provides security resources and educational content, but it doesn't actively monitor your digital infrastructure.

Coalition Control is a legitimate security tool:

- **Continuous vulnerability scanning** across your domains and IP ranges
- **Dark web monitoring** for exposed credentials linked to your agency
- **Risk scoring** that updates as your security posture changes
- **Automated alerts** when new CVEs affect software you're running
- **Security recommendations** prioritized by actual risk to your agency

For agencies that don't have a dedicated security team (which is most agencies under 50 people), Coalition Control fills a real gap. Several agency owners we spoke with said the monitoring alone justified the premium difference.

### 5. Claims Process

**Winner: Coalition**

Cyber incidents don't wait for business hours. Ransomware encrypts your files at 2 AM on a Saturday. A client discovers a data breach on a holiday weekend. When something goes wrong, response time matters — every hour of delay increases the cost and damage.

Coalition offers a **24/7 incident response hotline** staffed by pre-vetted forensics investigators, breach counsel, and crisis management professionals. When you call, you're connected with someone who can start working immediately — not an answering service that takes a message.

Embroker's incident response operates during **business hours**. For after-hours emergencies, you may face delays in getting a qualified response team engaged. For a solo freelancer, this might be acceptable. For an agency managing enterprise client data, it's a meaningful gap.

## Which One Should You Choose?

After testing both platforms extensively, here's our framework for deciding:

### Choose Embroker If:

- **You're a budget-conscious startup agency** — Every dollar matters when you're building, and Embroker's 20% savings add up
- **You're a solo freelancer or micro-agency** — The simpler coverage and lower price match your risk profile
- **You want fast, simple coverage** — Embroker's streamlined process gets you covered quickly without complexity
- **You already have security monitoring** — If you're running your own vulnerability scanning, you don't need to pay for Coalition Control
- **You're bundling multiple policies** — Embroker's packaging of cyber + E&O + general liability can simplify your insurance stack

### Choose Coalition If:

- **You want proactive security monitoring** — Coalition Control is genuinely useful, especially for agencies without dedicated security staff
- **You handle sensitive client data** — The broader coverage for social engineering and funds transfer fraud protects against the attacks that actually target agencies
- **Your clients require robust coverage** — Enterprise clients increasingly ask about your cyber insurance; Coalition's higher limits and broader terms satisfy more requirements
- **You need 24/7 incident response** — If a breach at 2 AM would be catastrophic for your client relationships, Coalition's always-on response team is worth the premium
- **You're a mid-size or larger agency** — As your agency grows, the value of Coalition's monitoring and broader coverage compounds

## Our Recommendation

For most digital agencies, **we recommend [Coalition](/compare/best-cyber-insurance-digital-agencies)**. The active monitoring through Coalition Control, broader coverage for social engineering and funds transfer fraud, and 24/7 incident response justify the roughly 20% premium over Embroker. When you factor in that Coalition Control replaces security monitoring tools that would cost $50–$200/month on their own, the effective price difference shrinks considerably.

That said, **[Embroker](/compare/best-cyber-insurance-digital-agencies)** is an excellent choice for startup agencies and freelancers where budget is the primary constraint. Their coverage is solid, their platform is genuinely good, and their understanding of tech companies is better than most legacy insurers.

The worst choice is no cyber insurance at all. With 43% of cyber attacks targeting small businesses and 60% of those businesses closing within six months of a significant attack, the question isn't whether you can afford cyber insurance — it's whether you can afford to go without it.
